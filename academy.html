Challenge Question
Scaling in 3 Dimensions
A small cube has a side length of 2 cm. A larger cube has a side length of 6 cm. Without calculating the exact volumes, how many times larger is the volume of the second cube compared to the first?
Correct Answer: D (27 times larger)

Explanation: The side length increases by a scale factor of 3 (6 ÷ 2 = 3). Because volume is a three-dimensional measurement (Length × Width × Height), the volume scales by the cube of that factor: 3³ = 3 × 3 × 3 = 27 times larger.
