Section A: Number Operations
10 Marks
Question 1.a
5,394 + 3,829 = ?
✓ Correct!
5,394 + 3,829 = 9,223. Break it down by place value: 4+9=13 (write 3, carry 1), 9+2+1=12 (write 2, carry 1), 3+8+1=12 (write 2, carry 1), 5+3+1=9.
Question 1.b
7,000 − 4,263 = ?
✓ Correct!
7,000 − 4,263 = 2,737. When subtracting from zeros, remember to borrow: 7000 = 6,999 + 1.
Question 1.c
356 × 14 = ?
✓ Correct!
356 × 14 = 4,984. Break it down: 356 × 4 = 1,424, then 356 × 10 = 3,560. Add: 1,424 + 3,560 = 4,984.
Question 1.d
1,944 ÷ 18 = ?
✓ Correct!
1,944 ÷ 18 = 108. Verify: 108 × 18 = 1,944 ✓
Question 1.e
4,200 ÷ 35 = ?
✓ Correct!
4,200 ÷ 35 = 120. Verify: 120 × 35 = 4,200 ✓
Question 1.f
84 × 99 = ?
✓ Correct!
84 × 99 = 84 × (100 - 1) = 8,400 - 84 = 8,316. Pro tip: Multiplying by 99 is the same as multiplying by 100 and subtracting once.
Section B: Fractions & Decimals
10 Marks
Question 2.a
Simplify: 18/24 = ?
✓ Correct!
18/24: Find the GCD of 18 and 24, which is 6. Divide both: 18÷6=3, 24÷6=4. So 18/24 = 3/4.
Question 2.b
Simplify: 30/45 = ?
✓ Correct!
30/45: The GCD is 15. Divide both: 30÷15=2, 45÷15=3. So 30/45 = 2/3.
Question 3.a
Calculate: 3/7 + 2/7 = ?
✓ Correct!
3/7 + 2/7 = (3+2)/7 = 5/7. When denominators are the same, just add the numerators.
Question 3.b
Calculate: 9/10 − 3/10 = ?
✓ Correct!
9/10 − 3/10 = (9-3)/10 = 6/10, which simplifies to 3/5.
Question 3.c
Calculate: 2/3 + 1/6 = ?
✓ Correct!
2/3 + 1/6: Find common denominator (6). Convert: 2/3 = 4/6. Then 4/6 + 1/6 = 5/6.
Question 4.a
Convert to decimal: 9/10 = ?
✓ Correct!
9/10 = 0.9. The denominator 10 means one decimal place: 9 ÷ 10 = 0.9.
Question 4.b
Convert to decimal: 47/100 = ?
✓ Correct!
47/100 = 0.47. The denominator 100 means two decimal places: 47 ÷ 100 = 0.47.
Question 5
Order from smallest to largest: 0.42, 0.402, 0.24, 0.204
✓ Correct!
Compare by looking at tenths first: 0.2 values come before 0.4. Then for 0.2 values: 0.204 < 0.24. For 0.4 values: 0.402 < 0.42.
Section C: Geometry & Measurement
10 Marks
Question 6
Find the perimeter of a rectangle with length 16 cm and width 7 cm.
✓ Correct!
Perimeter = 2(length + width) = 2(16 + 7) = 2(23) = 46 cm.
Question 7
Find the area of the rectangle from Question 6 (length 16 cm, width 7 cm).
✓ Correct!
Area = length × width = 16 × 7 = 112 cm².
Question 8.a
A square has a side length of 12 cm. Find its perimeter.
✓ Correct!
Perimeter of a square = 4 × side = 4 × 12 = 48 cm.
Question 8.b
A square has a side length of 12 cm. Find its area.
✓ Correct!
Area of a square = side × side = 12 × 12 = 144 cm².
Question 9
A triangle has two angles measuring 55° and 62°. Find the third angle.
✓ Correct!
Sum of angles in a triangle = 180°. Third angle = 180° - 55° - 62° = 63°.
Question 10
How many lines of symmetry does a regular octagon have?
✓ Correct!
A regular octagon has 8 lines of symmetry: 4 through opposite vertices and 4 through opposite sides.
Section D: Volume & Capacity (π = 3.14)
14 Marks
Question 11
A cylinder has a radius of 5 cm and height of 10 cm. Find its volume. (Use π = 3.14)
✓ Correct!
Volume of cylinder = πr²h = 3.14 × 5² × 10 = 3.14 × 25 × 10 = 785 cm³.
Question 12
A cone has a radius of 6 cm and height of 7 cm. Find its volume. (Use π = 3.14)
✓ Correct!
Volume of cone = (1/3)πr²h = (1/3) × 3.14 × 6² × 7 = (1/3) × 3.14 × 36 × 7 = 263.76 cm³.
Question 13
A sphere has a radius of 3 cm. Find its volume. (Use π = 3.14)
✓ Correct!
Volume of sphere = (4/3)πr³ = (4/3) × 3.14 × 3³ = (4/3) × 3.14 × 27 = 113.04 cm³.
Question 14
A cylindrical vase has a radius of 4 cm and height of 20 cm. How much water can it hold if filled completely? (Use π = 3.14)
✓ Correct!
Volume of cylinder = πr²h = 3.14 × 4² × 20 = 3.14 × 16 × 20 = 1,004.8 cm³.
Section E: Problem Solving
6 Marks
Question 15.a
A rectangular playground is 32 m long and 20 m wide. Find the total perimeter fence needed to enclose it.
✓ Correct!
Perimeter = 2(length + width) = 2(32 + 20) = 2(52) = 104 m.
Question 15.b
A rectangular playground is 32 m long and 20 m wide. Find the total area of the grass playground.
✓ Correct!
Area = length × width = 32 × 20 = 640 m².
Question 16
A cylindrical storage barrel has a radius of 10 cm and height of 30 cm. If it is filled halfway with oil, what is the volume of oil inside? (Use π = 3.14)
✓ Correct!
Full volume = πr²h = 3.14 × 10² × 30 = 3.14 × 100 × 30 = 9,420 cm³. Halfway = 9,420 ÷ 2 = 4,710 cm³.
Challenge Questions (Optional Bonus)
4 Bonus Marks
Question 17
A small cube has a side length of 2 cm. A larger cube has a side length of 6 cm. Without calculating the exact volumes, how many times larger is the volume of the second cube?
✓ Correct!
Scale factor = 6 ÷ 2 = 3. Volume scales by the cube of the linear factor: 3³ = 27 times larger.
Question 18
A cube has a total volume of 343 cm³. Find the side length of one of its edges.
✓ Correct!
Volume of cube = side³. So side = ∛343 = 7 cm. Verify: 7 × 7 × 7 = 343 ✓
